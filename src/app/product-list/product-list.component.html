Compression Spring

A type of spring designed to compress and become smaller when pressed with the compressive load. Compression spring normally refers to a coil compression spring.

Extension Spring

Induction coils are manufactured from copper enamelled wires or bare copper wires in cylindrical or parallel winding shape in single or multiple layers with stripped, tinned or moulded ends.

Garter Spring

Garter springs are long, close coiled extension springs with the ends fastened together and then used in the form of a ring to hold mechanical seals on a shaft or to hold round segments together.

Torison Spring

Torsion springs also known as constant force spring or Clock spring. This type of spring is usually a coiled ribbon of spring steel used for functions such as counter balancing, retrieving, maintaining constant loads, transmitting motion, and clamping.

Die Spring

Die springs are same as compression spring but made out of Flat wire which can be Square/Rectangular shape.It is categorised in different colour codes depending on its sizes and load capacity. We use chrome alloy material to manufacture this which are equivalent to ISO-10243 and JIS STD.
